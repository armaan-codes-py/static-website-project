TOP LANE

  • OBJECTIVES: Farming, Damage, Tanking, Flanking, and Split Pushing.

The top lane is one of the two solo lanes in League of Legends and is perfect for players who enjoy a good old one-versus-one brawl. Because of its isolated nature, Top Laners generally want to go down two paths. The first is to try and generate massive leads with fighters and the second is to sit tight and then show up big on tanks in teamfights where they can soak damage and provide plenty of Crowd Control.

Top Laners will often take the Teleport summoner spell, which enables them to teleport across the map. This means that they can split push in a side lane on the opposite side of the map to where the next objective will be contested, and then TP at the right time, or find flanks.

JUNGLE LANE

  • OBJECTIVES: Farming, Ganking, Protection, Smiting Objectives, and Vision Control.

Jungle is arguably the most unique role in League of Legends as instead of heading to one of the three lanes, the Jungler clears neutral monsters in the jungle and looks to influence different areas of the map. One of the things that Junglers can do is gank lanes, or in other words, try and surprise an enemy laner and kill them with a numbers advantage.

The summoner spell Smite is absolutely essential for Junglers as it allows them to buy a jungle item and execute normal monsters as well as larger objectives such as Dragon and Baron. Having Smite available for these objectives is vital for Junglers.

MIDS LANE

  • OBJECTIVES: Farming, Damage, Roaming, and Protecting Their Tower.

Mid Lane has always been a crucial role in League of Legends, as from its central position Mid Laners can influence the entire map if they want, or simply scale and be a major carry later on. With utility champions or assassins, players can look to farm their minions as quickly as possible and then rotate top or bot. As the shortest lane, mid is also safer for immobile mages who want to scale with both gold and solo experience. One of the goals in mid lane is to hold onto the tower for as long as possible because fallen mid lane towers open up the map for the enemy.

The mid lane does have one vital weakness though, as unlike top and bot, it has two open sides from which the enemy can gank. With this in mind, one of the many beginner tips for Mid Lane in League is to ward one side and then gravitate towards that side while laning.

BOT LANE

  • OBJECTIVES: Farming, Damage, DPS On Objectives.

League of Legends' bot lane is home to a duo made up of the Bot Lane Carry and a Support, and while mages and other champions can be played, the Bot Laner is usually an Attack Damage Carry, more commonly known as an ADC. These marksmen often scale extremely well into the game when they have bought enough items but are often weak early and are vulnerable to assassination and burst at all stages of the game.

The main aim of a bot laner is to get as much farm as possible in lane, and ideally enter the mid and late game stronger than their counterpart. In teamfights, ADCs should prioritize staying alive and slowly whittle down the enemy's frontline or any bigger threats who are in range. ADCs are also crucial for taking down large neutrals like Towers, Dragons, and Baron as they will usually have the most DPS.

SUPPORT

  • OBJECTIVES: Protecting, Engaging, Roaming, and Warding.

In the laning phase, the Support accompanies a carry in bot lane and helps them farm, stay safe and ideally get some kills too. Supports do not take farm for themselves and thus have a limited income, which their support item helps with. Support champions come in a variety of forms from enchanters who excel at healing, shielding, and otherwise keeping allies alive, to engage tanks who want to set up plays and everything in between.

Contrary to the belief of some Bot Lane Carries though, Supports do not exist primarily for them, but for the entire team. This means that Supports can look to roam during the laning phase, and if other allies are in better positions to carry, then helping them do so will be more beneficial. Additionally, even though every team member has a responsibility to contribute towards vision, Supports have more access to wards from their support item and thus have the greatest responsibility for placing and destroying wards.
